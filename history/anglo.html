    • William securing the Kingdom

      • Establishing Control

        • Submission of the Earls: The other Earls did not fight William because:

          • He had seized royal treasury
          • Threatened to cut London off supplies
          • England's best warriors died at the battle

        • Rewarding followers: He sent lavish gifts to the pope. Set a heavy geld tax to pay his mercenaries. He declared all land in England was owned by him and granted land and earldoms to his followers.

        • Castles: He built castles to control Anglo-Saxons in the local area. They were located in strategically important places. Can surveillance people. They were used at the base for attacks. It also tried to make the Saxons feel dominated.

          • Features: They would have 2 moats filled with water around the castle. They would have one entrances to the area, via a bridge. The main castle would be separate to the village. There would also be walls and towers surrounding it.

      • Anglo-Saxon resistance

        • Edwin and Morcar(1068): With taxes rising, geld tax, and the loss of land, Morcar was going to revolt. He believed the government, Bishop Odo, had claimed land unlawfully, and Edwin was unhappy as he didn't marry Williams daughter. Edwin and Morcar submitted to William and William pardoned them.

          • Consequences: William decided to put Robert Cumin(a Norman) in charge of the north.

        • Uprising in York(1069): Uprising in york happens and Edgar Aethling comes and joins in from Scotland. William attacks and defeats them easily. Edgar goes back to Scotland.

        • Anglo-Danish attack on York(1069): King Sweyn from Denmark sends an invasion. They meet up with Edgar in York. 21st September 3000 Normans are killed. The Danes retreat to Lincolnshire coast. Revolts started to happen throughout the country. To stop this William pays the Danes and does the Harrying of the North.

        • Hereward the Wake and Ely(1070): Hereward returned to Ely to find his land was taken away. He started a rebellion. In 1070 the Danes returned and set up base in Ely. They joined forces and raided Peterbourgh Abbey. He hoped to save the treasures but the Danes took them and went back to Denmark. Morcar and his men came to Ely and joined Hereward. They defended Ely but the Normans eventually defeated them. Morcar was captured and Hereward escaped...

    • Anglo Saxon England

      • Anglo-Saxon Society

        • Power and control: The King was in charge and had the most power. In charge of lawmaking, money, tax, religion, landownership and the army. The Witan was a council who would give advice to the king. The earls were most important ment in the country. Oversee law over large areas.

        • Economy: The economy was strong in England due to the good climate for farming. It had strong trade links. The tax was very efficient, which made earls and the king rich. There was also centralised control of money.

        • Society: There was 2 million people in England and only 4000-6000 were 9.7. 10% if the population were slaves.

        • Church: Bishops were rich and important. Controlled the church. Not many people were literate, mainly the church was. Priests were poor and were farmers. True for monks and nuns.

      • Last Years of Edward

        • Power of Godwins: The Godwins owned most of the earldoms. They had more money than the king and land. The Godwins were also connected to the King through Edwards wife. In 1050, Edward tried to free himself from Godwins dominance. Edward could have gone to Normandy to promise William the throne.

          • Harold Godwinson: He had great success. Owning more land than the Kind, having more money than the King and also being best mates with the king. Edward trusted Harold loads.

        • Harolds embassy: Edward wanted to send a message to William so he sent Harold. Harold got taken prisoner and William rescued him. Harold thought for William and reward him with gifts. Harold swore a very serious oath as part of the embassy but we dont know what that is.

          • Importance: Shows that Edward trusted Harold. Normans believe it was Edward telling William he will have the throne. 'Legitimising' it. Normans used this to say Harold is an oath breaker.

        • Tostig: October 1065 Northumbrian thegns revolt against Tostig. They invite Morcar to become Earl. By 1st November Tostig is exiled. This was due to Tostig abusing and going too far on his crackdown of lawlessness and abusing his men.

        • Death of Edward: Edward dies but does not give a heir to the throne. Edwards funeral was on 6th January 1066.

        • William of Normandy: Agreement from Edward following Harolds embassy. Backed by pope but is lacking evidence. Good military success.

        • Battle of Gate Fulford: Hardrada and Tostig invaded. Morcar and Edwin defended. Had an open battle. Edwin and Morcar were outnumbered. Load of english men were killed. Harold were forced to come up and help.

        • Battle of Stamford Bridge: Harolds army surprised the Biking army. Harold was victorious and killed Harald and and Tostig. This was due to the Vikings leaving their armour on the ship.

          • Significance: Recieved great achivary. However William did invade the south whilst that was happening. Harold had to rapidly move south.
